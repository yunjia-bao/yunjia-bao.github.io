Tablinum

Everyone needs a small pavilion to collect the shells and petals that they picked along the way of an adventure. Tablinum is the Latin word for a room that is similar to a study or an office. It was usually the house master's center of business. But I intent to use this page as a shareable ground for every visitor on the work on which I was, am, have been, or will be working. As a scholar, I believe that I should not only treat myself with the delight of knowledge but also illustrate and explain to more people why the field of study I love is relevant and even intriguing to them. Scholars should both create knowledge and share the joy from the knowledge and joy of creating knowledge to more people. Believing that internet is the most expansive one of all means of communication and sharing, I will place most of my private work, my random ideas, my notes, my programs, etc. on this page and its subpages for people to enjoy.

I believe that a scholar should always try to grant as much access to readers on the knowledge they created as possible. Thus, most of the contents will be open-access and effectively free-to-adapt under some creative commons license, but there are certain restrictions on reuse and adaptation. Others may not be allowed for any kind of derivatives or commercial usage. Therefore, please follow the license accordingly while you are experiencing the delight of knowledge.

Notes and Thoughts  
  • Contour Integral Made Easy: This is a note on complex contour integrals, especially on the famous residue theorem, from the perspective of a physicist. That said, this paper intends to tease the readers, pave a simple but elegant picture, and invite the readers to explore more mathematical subtleties about contour integrals and complex analysis in general. This note can also be used as a quick refresher on the theorem before applying it for it records one version of the theorem without proving it.
  • Clifford Algebras: This is a note on Clifford algebras as "quadratic algebras" with the idea of quadratic spaces and quadratic forms (for more information, see the note below). With these abstract algebraic structures, this paper intends to balance the mathematical rigor and the intuitive picture. As opposed to the previous note on contour integrals, this note is slightly more technical in order to show readers the complexity of this "simple" algebraic construction over a vector space with a notion of "squared length".
  • Quadratic Space: This is a note on quadratic spaces as "generalized inner product space" with the notion of equivalence with respect to coordinate transformations. With these abstract algebraic structures, this paper intends to provide some background pictures for the ``Clifford algebras'' note. This note also include some useful properties of direct sums and tensor products of vector/quadratic spaces and how these operations eventually leads to a commutative ring structure that is computationally useful for identifying the equivalence (isometry) classes of quadratic forms.
