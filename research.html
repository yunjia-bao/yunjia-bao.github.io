Research Interest

As a master's student in physics, I am open to all possibilities in my research experience as I am aware of my naiveté in physics and would love to explore more professional techniques and research topics. I have been exposed to the study of dynamical systems for two summers and the study of high-energy particle phenomenology for one summer and realized that physics is indeed a field of study which deeply entices me. I also notice that I am inclined to understand the physical world from a more abstract and theoretical perspective. Therefore, I am switching my main focus to a more theoretical side of physics, but I am still actively learning about experimental developments and techniques.

Recently, I started working on direct construction and understanding of scattering amplitudes, also known as the S-matrices, used in particle experiments. This field of study asks the question about the connection between the local Lorentz and gauge constraints and the global observables that we probe at infinity in a scattering experiment. It investigates the possibility of reducing the evaluation of factorially more local Feynman diagrams to diagrams in which locality does not manifest but can be enforced. Efficient calculation is for what I am aiming, but there may be deep insights to the development of quantum field theory from this idea about shifting the manifestation of one property to another.

Since I am in the middle of a critical transition of my academic life, I am open to all forms of suggestions, advice, and criticisms from all sources on my choices of field of interest. If you have some research topic which you find quite interesting, please feel free to share it with me.
